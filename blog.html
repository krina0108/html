India Seeks Bigger, More Frequent
Dividends From State-Run Firms

By Siddhartha Singh

June 2, 2025 at 3:12 PM GMT+5:30

save Translate listen

The Indian government is asking state-run companies to increase
dividend payouts by about 25% this financial year as Asia’s third-
largest economy seeks to bolster finances in a volatile global
environment, according to people with knowledge of the matter.


Officials from the Department of Investment and Public Asset
Management are also meeting company executives to request that that
these payments be made quarterly rather than annually, the
people said, asking not to be identified as the information isn’t
public. The government wants to garner about 900 billion
rupees ($10.5 billion) through dividends in the year through March 2026,
the people added, compared with 740.2 billion rupees received in
the previous year.
